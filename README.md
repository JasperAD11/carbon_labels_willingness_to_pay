# Humanized Carbon Labels and Willingness to Pay for Flights

This repository contains our group project for the **Causality and Randomized Experiments** course at **Católica**, submitted in March 2025.

## Project overview

The project studies whether the way carbon emissions are presented in a flight-booking setting affects a traveler’s **willingness to pay (WTP)** for a lower-emission flight.

We compare two framing conditions:

- **Control:** carbon emissions shown as raw numerical information
- **Treatment:** carbon emissions shown in a more humanized and relatable way

The study is based on a randomized survey experiment designed around a flight booking scenario inspired by platforms such as Google Flights. :contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0} :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}

## Research question

**Does humanized framing of carbon emissions affect willingness to pay for lower-emission flights?** :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}

## Main finding

The treatment effect was small and not statistically significant.

- Mean WTP in control group: **12.16**
- Mean WTP in treatment group: **12.29**
- Estimated average treatment effect: **0.13**

Overall, the results suggest that humanized carbon framing did **not** meaningfully increase willingness to pay in this study. :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3} :contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4}
